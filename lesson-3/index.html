📖 Band 8.0 Model Report

The table illustrates the number of internet users, measured in millions, across five countries — the USA, UK, China, India, and Brazil — over a twenty-year period from 2000 to 2020.

Overall, the most striking feature is the exponential surge[adj+noun] in internet adoption in China and India, which vastly outpaced the more gradual increases observed in the other three nations. By 2020, China had emerged as the country with the largest user base.

In 2000, China and India had relatively modest numbers of internet users, at 22 million and 5 million respectively. However, both countries experienced dramatic growth over the following two decades. China's figures skyrocketed[Band 8 verb] to 989 million by 2020, while India's user base soared from[verb+prep] 5 million to 749 million, representing the most significant proportional increase among the five countries.

In contrast, the USA, UK, and Brazil demonstrated more measured growth. The USA climbed steadily from 95 million to 300 million. Brazil showed moderate expansion, rising from 5 million to 165 million. Meanwhile, the UK's growth tapered off[phrasal verb] after 2015, with the figure edging up marginally[precision collocation] from 60 million to just 63 million, suggesting that the market had reached saturation.
