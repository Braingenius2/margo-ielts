Fluency & Coherence

Lexical Resource

Grammatical Range

Pronunciation
Use a range of features (stress, intonation, rhythm). Easy to understand throughout.

⏱ Topic 1
1:30
Band 7+: Well, I'm currently in my third year at university — I'm studying International Relations. To be honest, it wasn't what I initially planned to do, but I've really grown to love it. What I enjoy most is probably the way it's opened my eyes to how interconnected the world actually is. You know, we tend to see things from our own perspective, but studying IR forces you to consider multiple viewpoints, which I find fascinating.
🗣 Topic Set 2: Ambition & Goals
1. What are your ambitions for the future?
2. Do you think it's important to set goals? Why?
3. Who has influenced your ambitions most?
⏱ Topic 2
1:30
Band 7+: That's a good question. My main ambition, I'd say, is to build a career in diplomacy or international development. I think setting goals is absolutely crucial — without them, you can kind of drift through life without any real direction. As for who's influenced me most, I'd have to say my parents — they've always encouraged me to aim high, but they've also taught me that success isn't just about career achievements. I mean, it's about being a decent person along the way.
🗣 Topic Set 3: Defining Success
1. How do you define success in life?
2. Do you think successful people share common qualities?
3. Is it more important to be successful or to be happy?
⏱ Topic 3
1:30
Band 7+: You know, I've actually thought about this quite a lot. For me personally, success isn't really about money or status — I mean, those things are nice to have, but they're not what I'd consider true success. I think real success is about personal fulfilment and making some kind of positive impact, however small. And interestingly, I've noticed that people who are genuinely successful — they all seem to share qualities like resilience, humility, and a willingness to keep learning.
