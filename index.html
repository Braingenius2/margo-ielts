IELTS Academic Exam Structure

Total test time: 2 hours 45 minutes. Listening, Reading, and Writing are taken on the same day with no breaks between them. The Speaking test may be on the same day or up to 7 days before/after.

Section Time Questions Details
Listening ~30 min
(+10 min transfer)		 40 4 recordings (2 monologues, 2 conversations) in varying accents. Played only once.
Reading 60 minutes 40 3 long academic passages from books, journals, and newspapers. Various question types (matching headings, gap-fill, short answer, multiple choice).
Writing 60 minutes 2 tasks Task 1: Describe a graph, chart, table, or diagram (150 words, ~20 min)

Task 2: Write an essay responding to an argument or problem (250 words, ~40 min). Worth 2x Task 1.
Speaking 11-14 min 3 parts Face-to-face with a trained examiner.

Part 1: Intro & general questions (4-5 min)
Part 2: Cue card long turn — 1 min prep + 2 min speak (3-4 min)
Part 3: Abstract discussion on Part 2 topic (4-5 min)
Critical Notes
·
No breaks between Listening, Reading, Writing: These three sections run consecutively. Make sure to manage your time carefully, especially in Writing.
·
Face-to-face Speaking: Unlike TOEFL, IELTS Speaking is conducted with a real examiner in a private room, not a computer. This allows for a more natural conversation flow.
·
Task 2 is worth double: The Writing Task 2 essay contributes twice as much to your Writing band score as Task 1. Prioritise quality on the essay.
