IELTS Academic Exam Structure

Total test time: 2 hours 45 minutes. Listening, Reading, and Writing are taken on the same day with no breaks between them. The Speaking test may be on the same day or up to 7 days before/after.

Section Time Questions Details
Listening ~30 min
(+10 min transfer)		 40 4 recordings (2 monologues, 2 conversations) in varying accents. Played only once.
Reading 60 minutes 40 3 long academic passages from books, journals, and newspapers.
Writing 60 minutes 2 tasks Task 1: Describe a graph/chart (150 words, ~20 min)

Task 2: Essay (250 words, ~40 min). Worth 2x Task 1.
Speaking 11-14 min 3 parts Face-to-face with a trained examiner. Part 1: Intro (4-5 min) | Part 2: Cue card (3-4 min) | Part 3: Abstract discussion (4-5 min)
Critical Notes
No breaks between Listening, Reading, Writing: These three sections run consecutively. Manage your time carefully, especially in Writing.
Face-to-face Speaking: Unlike TOEFL, IELTS Speaking is conducted with a real examiner in a private room, not a computer.
Task 2 is worth double: The Writing Task 2 essay contributes twice as much to your Writing band score as Task 1.
